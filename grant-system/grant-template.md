# Grant Templates - Reusable Components

## Program Description (Reusable)
The Pre-Apprenticeship Certificate Training (PACT) program is a national skills training model that integrates work-based learning with vocational and academic skills training, and includes job readiness, employability skills, on-the-job training and job placement.

**Target Population:** Court-referred youth ages 16-17 in Mecklenburg County

**Program Components:**
- Hands-on construction vocational training
- Academic skills (PACTMath, PACTCommunication)
- Evidence-based curricula: Life Skills Training, Guiding Good Choices
- Job placement services
- Parent engagement workshops

---

## Problem Statement (From JCPC)
According to the 2016-17 Needs Assessment Data for Mecklenburg County:
- **59.9%** of youth associate with delinquent peers or gang members
- **77.1%** have moderate to serious school problems
- **59.3%** of parents have marginal parenting skills
- **37.5%** of youth live with family members with criminal records

These youth face significant barriers: high-poverty environments, fragmented families, school disconnection, and lack of employment opportunities. Without intervention, these risk factors lead to continued delinquent behavior and limited future options.

**The Need:** Court-referred youth need positive role models, vocational training, and pathways to employment to reduce recidivism and build sustainable futures.

---

## Solution Narrative (Reusable)
Give N Go has adopted the Pre-Apprenticeship Certificate Training (PACT) model, which has been granted the status of nationally recognized credential by the U.S. Department of Labor.

Our approach:
- **25%** classroom instruction (academic skills, life skills curriculum)
- **75%** hands-on work-based learning (construction trades, community service)

**Evidence-Based Curricula:**
- Life Skills Training (LST) — prevents substance use, builds self-esteem
- Guiding Good Choices — parent engagement, family communication

**Expected Outcomes:**
- High school completion or GED progress
- Industry-recognized PACT certificate
- Job placement in construction trades
- Reduced recidivism
